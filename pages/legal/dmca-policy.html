1. What to Consider Before Submitting a Copyright Complaint

Before submitting a copyright complaint, please consider:

  • Whether the use qualifies as fair use under copyright law
  • Whether you hold the copyright or are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner
  • The accuracy of your claim
Important: The DMCA requires you to provide personal information in your copyright infringement notification. If you're concerned about privacy, you may wish to hire an agent to submit the notification on your behalf.

If you're unsure whether material infringes your copyright, we recommend consulting with an attorney before filing a notification.

2. Notification of Infringement Process

If you believe content on our Website infringes your copyright, you may submit a written DMCA notification that includes:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed
  3. Identification of the infringing material with sufficient detail for us to locate it
  4. Your contact information (address, telephone number, and email)
  5. A statement that you have a good faith belief the use is unauthorized
  6. A statement that the information is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you're authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner

Our Response

Upon receiving a valid DMCA notice:

  • We will review the complaint for accuracy, validity, and completeness
  • We may remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material
  • We will notify the affected user of the removal
  • We may terminate repeat infringers' accounts

3. Counter-Notification Process

If you believe your content was wrongly removed due to a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notification containing:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature
  2. Identification of the removed content and its location before removal
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you believe the content was removed by mistake
  4. Your name, address, phone number, and consent to jurisdiction in federal court

Upon receiving a valid counter-notification, we may restore the removed content within 10-14 business days unless we receive notice that legal action has been filed.

4. Changes to This Policy

We may modify this Policy at any time. When we do, we will:

  • Update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page
  • Post a notice on our Website's main page
  • Notify users through email if appropriate

Your continued use of our Website after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.
